# 07 — AI-Powered Productivity Workflows

AI tools can dramatically reduce time spent on repetitive cognitive tasks. This guide provides workflow blueprints you can adapt to your role.

---

## The Core Productivity Loop

```
1. CAPTURE  →  Dump raw info into the AI (notes, transcripts, data)
2. PROCESS  →  Ask AI to organize, summarize, or transform it
3. REVIEW   →  Read and edit the AI output
4. ACT      →  Use the polished output in your work
```

Spending 30 seconds writing a good prompt can save 30 minutes of work.

---

## Workflow 1: Meeting Preparation

**Time saved: 15–30 min per meeting**

1. Paste the meeting agenda into Claude/ChatGPT
2. Prompt: *"Generate 5 smart questions I should ask, and list any potential risks or blockers to raise."*
3. Prompt: *"Summarize what I need to know going into this meeting based on this agenda."*

---

## Workflow 2: Meeting Debrief & Notes

**Time saved: 20–45 min per meeting**

1. Paste raw transcript or bullet notes into the AI
2. Prompt: *"Organize these notes into: Key Decisions, Action Items (with owners), Open Questions, and Next Steps."*
3. Prompt: *"Write a 3-sentence summary I can paste into Slack for the team."*

---

## Workflow 3: Email Triage & Drafting

**Time saved: 30–60 min per day**

1. Paste a long email thread into the AI
2. Prompt: *"Summarize this thread. What is being asked of me and by when?"*
3. Prompt: *"Draft a professional reply that [agrees / declines / asks for clarification]. Keep it under 100 words."*

---

## Workflow 4: Document Review & Feedback

**Time saved: 30–90 min per document**

1. Paste the document text into the AI
2. Prompt: *"Review this document for clarity, structure, and any logical gaps. Give me specific suggestions."*
3. Prompt: *"Rewrite the introduction to be more engaging for a non-technical audience."*
4. Prompt: *"Generate an executive summary in 5 bullet points."*

---

## Workflow 5: Weekly Planning

**Time saved: 20–30 min per week**

1. Share your task list, calendar, and goals with the AI
2. Prompt: *"Given these tasks and deadlines, suggest a prioritized plan for the week. Highlight any conflicts."*
3. Prompt: *"What should I defer or delegate given this workload?"*

---

## Workflow 6: Research & Synthesis

**Time saved: 1–3 hours per research task**

1. Collect 3–5 articles or documents
2. Paste key excerpts into the AI (mind privacy — no confidential sources)
3. Prompt: *"Identify the common themes, key disagreements, and most important takeaways across these sources."*
4. Prompt: *"What questions are NOT answered by these sources that I should investigate further?"*

---

## Workflow 7: Presentation Building

**Time saved: 1–2 hours per deck**

1. Share your topic, audience, and key message
2. Prompt: *"Create a slide-by-slide outline for a 10-minute presentation on [topic] for [audience]. Include the key point and supporting data for each slide."*
3. Prompt: *"Write speaker notes for slide 3."*
4. Prompt: *"Suggest 3 strong opening hooks for this presentation."*

---

## Automation Ideas

Once you're comfortable with AI prompting, explore these next-level productivity moves:

- **Custom instructions / system prompts** — Pre-set your role, tone, and preferences so you don't repeat context each time
- **Prompt libraries** — Save your best-performing prompts in a Notion page or this repo
- **AI + Zapier / Make** — Automate workflows: e.g., summarize emails, post meeting notes to Slack
- **Batch processing** — Feed multiple similar items to the AI in one go (e.g., 10 product descriptions at once)

---

## Productivity Anti-Patterns

| Mistake | Better Approach |
|---------|----------------|
| Accepting the first output | Always iterate at least once |
| Using AI for tasks you're faster at | Reserve AI for cognitively heavy or time-consuming tasks |
| Not reviewing AI output | You're responsible for everything you send |
| Over-prompting (too many constraints) | Start simple, add constraints only if needed |
