# 05 — Safety & Ethics in AI Usage

Using AI responsibly is not just a technical concern — it's an ethical one. This guide outlines key principles and practical rules for safe, fair, and transparent AI usage.

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Human Oversight
AI should assist humans, not replace human judgment in high-stakes decisions. Always review AI outputs before acting on them — especially in legal, medical, financial, or people-related contexts.

### 2. Transparency
Be transparent about your use of AI. If an AI-generated text will be published, presented, or sent externally, disclose this where appropriate per your organization's or industry's norms.

### 3. Accuracy Over Speed
AI can generate plausible-sounding but incorrect information (see "hallucinations" in [10-glossary.md](./10-glossary.md)). Verify important facts, figures, citations, and code before using them.

### 4. Fairness and Bias Awareness
AI models can reflect biases present in their training data. Be alert to outputs that:
- Stereotype groups of people
- Favor certain demographics
- Make assumptions based on gender, ethnicity, or geography

Always apply a critical lens, especially when AI is used in hiring, performance evaluation, or customer segmentation.

### 5. Do No Harm
Never use AI to:
- Generate misleading or manipulative content
- Impersonate real people
- Create or spread disinformation
- Harass, discriminate against, or harm individuals or groups

---

## Responsible Use Checklist

Before using AI output in a professional or public context:

- [ ] Have I reviewed the output for accuracy?
- [ ] Have I checked for potential bias or unfair framing?
- [ ] Is this output appropriate for the intended audience?
- [ ] Am I disclosing AI use where required or expected?
- [ ] Have I avoided sharing sensitive personal or confidential data with the AI?
- [ ] Is a human making the final decision in high-stakes situations?

---

## High-Risk Use Cases — Proceed with Caution

These areas require extra scrutiny and human review:

| Area | Risk | Recommended Action |
|------|------|-------------------|
| Hiring & HR | Bias in candidate evaluation | Human review mandatory |
| Legal documents | Inaccurate or incomplete legal advice | Consult a licensed attorney |
| Medical information | Dangerous if incorrect | Consult a qualified professional |
| Financial decisions | May lack current market data | Verify with financial advisors |
| Code in production | Security vulnerabilities | Full code review required |
| Public communications | Reputational risk | Editorial review before publishing |

---

## AI and Copyright

- AI-generated content may incorporate patterns from copyrighted works
- Ownership of AI-generated content varies by jurisdiction — check local laws
- Do not use AI to reproduce copyrighted text, music, or art
- For commercial use, prefer AI tools with clear licensing terms (e.g., Adobe Firefly for images)

---

## Organizational AI Policy

Your organization may have specific policies on:
- Which AI tools are approved for use
- What data can be entered into AI systems
- How AI use should be disclosed in deliverables
- Who owns AI-generated work

**If in doubt, ask your manager or IT/legal team before using AI on a work task.**
