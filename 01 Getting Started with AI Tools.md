 01 — Getting Started with AI Tools

## What Is AI?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to systems that can perform tasks typically requiring human intelligence — such as understanding language, generating text, analyzing data, writing code, or creating images.

Modern AI tools like Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, and Copilot are powered by **Large Language Models (LLMs)** — systems trained on massive amounts of text to understand and generate human language.

---

## How Do AI Assistants Work?

AI assistants operate on a simple principle:

1. **You send a message** (called a "prompt")
2. **The model processes it** using patterns learned during training
3. **It generates a response** — token by token, predicting what comes next

They do **not** browse the internet in real time (unless explicitly told to) and do **not** have memory between conversations unless that feature is enabled.

---

## Core Concepts to Know

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Prompt** | Your input to the AI — a question, instruction, or task |
| **Response / Completion** | The AI's output |
| **Context window** | How much text (conversation history + prompt) the AI can "see" at once |
| **Temperature** | Controls randomness — lower = more predictable, higher = more creative |
| **System prompt** | Background instructions that shape the AI's behavior |
| **Token** | The basic unit of text the model processes (~¾ of a word) |

## Choosing the Right Tool

Different AI tools are suited to different tasks:

- **Claude (Anthropic)** — Excellent for reasoning, writing, analysis, and long documents
- **ChatGPT (OpenAI)** — Versatile general-purpose assistant
- **Gemini (Google)** — Strong integration with Google Workspace
- **GitHub Copilot** — Specialized for code completion in IDEs
- **Midjourney / DALL·E** — Image generation

---

## Your First Prompt — Tips

- Be **specific** about what you want
- Give **context** ("I'm a marketing manager writing to a B2B audience...")
- Specify the **format** ("Give me a bullet list" or "Write this as a short email")
- Iterate — if the first answer isn't right, **ask for a revision**

### Example

❌ Weak prompt:
> "Write something about AI."

✅ Strong prompt:
> "Write a 3-paragraph introduction to AI tools for non-technical employees at a mid-sized company. Keep the tone friendly and avoid jargon."
